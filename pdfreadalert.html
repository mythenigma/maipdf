MaiPDF ReadNotify: Get Notified When Your PDF Link Is Opened

Track your PDF documents and get notified via email when someone accesses your shared PDF link.

What is MaiPDF ReadNotify?

MaiPDF's ReadNotify feature allows you to receive an email notification when a recipient opens the PDF document that you’ve shared as a link. This feature ensures that you are always informed when your document is being accessed, adding an extra layer of security and control.

By simply entering an email address during the PDF link generation process, MaiPDF will automatically send an email notification to that address whenever the PDF link is clicked and opened by someone else.

How Does ReadNotify Work?

When you generate a PDF link using MaiPDF, you will be prompted to input an email address. Once you've entered the email address and the recipient opens the PDF link, the system will send a notification to that email, informing you of the following details:

  • When the link was accessed — Get the exact timestamp of when the document was opened.
  • Who accessed the document — You will be informed of the device or IP address (depending on the security settings).
  • Document access tracking — Gain insights into how and when your document is being accessed, helping you track its usage.

Why Should You Use ReadNotify?

The ReadNotify feature is ideal for anyone who needs to monitor document access or track the distribution of sensitive information. Some of the benefits include:

  • Document Security: Ensure that your documents are only being viewed by the intended recipient. Receive alerts if the document is opened unexpectedly.
  • Improved Tracking: If you're sharing contracts, proposals, or confidential reports, knowing when and how your document is accessed can be crucial for compliance and accountability.
  • Peace of Mind: Keep track of your documents remotely, knowing that you will be notified immediately when someone opens your PDF link.

Who Can Benefit from ReadNotify?

ReadNotify is beneficial for a variety of users who need to keep track of their documents and control access. Here are some examples:

  • Businesses and Corporations: Protect confidential contracts, legal documents, and sensitive reports by knowing exactly when they are accessed.
  • Freelancers and Consultants: Track when clients view project proposals or contracts, ensuring accountability and transparency.
  • Legal Professionals: Keep an eye on when legal documents are opened and ensure they are handled according to protocols.
  • Educational Institutions: Monitor when important academic papers, research, or exams are accessed by students or other parties.
