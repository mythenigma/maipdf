Zero-Width Characters and Information Encryption

Zero-width characters are special types of characters that occupy space in text without displaying any visible symbol. These characters are used to hide information or mark parts of text without affecting the appearance of the text itself. They are particularly useful in encryption and steganography applications.

What Are Zero-Width Characters?

Zero-width characters are a class of characters with specific Unicode encodings (e.g., U+200B for Zero Width Space) that do not visually appear in text but still occupy space. While invisible to the reader, they can carry hidden information that can be retrieved with the appropriate tools or algorithms.

Common Zero-Width Characters:

  • Zero-Width Space (ZWSP, U+200B): The most common zero-width character used to insert an invisible space in text without affecting its readability.
  • Zero-Width Non-Joiner (ZWNJ, U+200C): Prevents the joining of characters, commonly used in languages such as Arabic and Persian.
  • Zero-Width Joiner (ZWJ, U+200D): Forces characters to join, used in certain writing systems to combine characters.
  • Zero-Width Non-Breaking Space (ZWNBSP, U+2060): Prevents a line break at a specific point in text.

Applications of Zero-Width Characters:

Zero-width characters, due to their invisibility, are used in various fields, especially in encryption and steganography:

  • Steganography: Zero-width characters are often used to hide information in plain text, making the message invisible to the casual observer but retrievable through specialized tools.
  • Encryption: Zero-width characters can be used as containers to hide encrypted information, ensuring that only those with the proper decryption key can access the hidden message.
  • Preventing Data Leakage: By embedding sensitive information in zero-width characters, data leakage can be prevented, and unauthorized access can be avoided.

Zero-Width Characters and Privacy Protection:

In the age of digital communication, privacy and security are major concerns. Zero-width characters provide an effective and hidden way to protect sensitive information. By embedding data in regular text, zero-width characters allow for secure communication without revealing the hidden information to unauthorized users.
