MaiPDF vs. Traditional DRM Solutions

What is Adobe Content Server?

Adobe Content Server is a server-controlled approach to Digital Rights Management (DRM) introduced by Adobe for PDF documents (DRM PDFs). It was developed to protect digital books in formats such as PDF, EPUB, and EPUB3. However, Adobe’s solution is often expensive, and its reliance on specific software can be a limitation for end-users.

Additionally, Adobe Content Server’s model requires licensing fees, making it costly for small businesses and individuals to use.

Locklizard and Digify

Locklizard and Digify offer secure file sharing solutions with DRM and viewing restrictions. However, these platforms require users to install proprietary software or rely on specific file formats (like PDC for Locklizard). This limits flexibility and compatibility with various devices and platforms.

Unlike Adobe and Digify, MaiPDF allows you to share protected PDFs in a webview format without needing any additional software installation. It's an easy-to-use, flexible, and free solution for all users.
